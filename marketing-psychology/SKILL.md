# Marketing Psychology Frameworks

## Core Frameworks

### Hook-Story-Offer
1. **Hook** ‚Äî Grab attention in 3 seconds
2. **Story** ‚Äî Build emotional connection
3. **Offer** ‚Äî Present the transformation

### Problem ‚Üí Promise ‚Üí Proof ‚Üí Proposition
1. **Problem** ‚Äî Agitate the pain point
2. **Promise** ‚Äî Present your solution
3. **Proof** ‚Äî Social proof, case studies, testimonials
4. **Proposition** ‚Äî Clear call-to-action

### 7 Psychological Triggers
1. **Scarcity** ‚Äî Limited time/availability
2. **Urgency** ‚Äî Act now or miss out
3. **Social Proof** ‚Äî Others have chosen this
4. **Authority** ‚Äî Expert endorsement
5. **Reciprocity** ‚Äî Give value first
6. **Risk Reversal** ‚Äî Guarantees, no-risk trials
7. **Liking** ‚Äî Relatable, trustworthy brand voice

### Voice & Tone
- Direct and blunt ‚Äî no sugarcoating
- Results-focused ‚Äî always tie back to revenue
- "Apply, don't book" ‚Äî strong CTAs
- Position the business as "the prize"

## Copy Audit Checklist

- [ ] Headline is a strong hook (<10 words)
- [ ] Problem is clearly identified and agitated
- [ ] Solution is presented as the obvious choice
- [ ] Social proof is visible on the page
- [ ] CTA is direct and action-oriented
- [ ] Risk is reversed (guarantee, free consultation)
- [ ] Copy is benefit-focused, not feature-focused
- [ ] Each page has ONE primary CTA
- [ ] Trust signals visible (badges, reviews, logos)
- [ ] Scarcity/urgency used ethically where applicable
